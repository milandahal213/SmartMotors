  1. Automated Plant Sunlight Tracker:
    • Have students create a device that tracks the sunlight throughout the day to keep a potted plant oriented towards the light source. They can program the servo motor to move based on the light sensor readings, ensuring that the plant receives optimal sunlight.
  1. Solar-Powered Art Display:
    • Challenge students to design an art display that moves in response to light levels. They can create different shapes or patterns with the servo motor, and the light sensor will dictate the movement. This project combines artistic creativity with programming skills.
  1. Day-Night Simulation:
    • Ask students to simulate a day-night cycle using their kits. The servo motor can control a rotating element (representing the sun), and the light sensor can trigger the motor to move based on changing light conditions, creating a simple but effective day-night cycle model.
  1. Interactive Storytelling Device:
    • Have students build a storytelling device where the servo motor positions a character or object based on the intensity of light detected by the sensor. They can program a sequence of movements to tell a story that unfolds as the lighting conditions change.
  1. Weather Station Prototype:
    • In this project, students can design a miniature weather station. The servo motor can adjust a "weather vane" while the light sensor measures ambient light. They can correlate movements to weather conditions, providing a simple representation of wind direction.
  1. Musical Light Show:
    • Challenge students to create a light-based musical show where the servo motor moves in sync with the light sensor readings. Assign different positions to musical notes or beats, allowing students to program their own light-based musical compositions.
  1. Shadow Puppet Theater:
    • Challenge students to create a shadow puppet theater using the servo motor to control the position of shadow puppets. The light sensor can be used to trigger specific puppet movements based on the intensity of light, allowing for an interactive storytelling experience.
  1. Intruder Alert System:
    • Have students design a security system using the light sensor to detect changes in ambient light. The servo motor can control a mechanism that alerts when the light suddenly increases or decreases, simulating an intruder alert system.
  1. Solar Panel Angle Optimizer:
    • Task students with building a model of a solar panel system. The servo motor can adjust the angle of the solar panel based on the intensity of sunlight measured by the light sensor, optimizing the panel's position for maximum energy absorption.
  1. Morse Code Communicator:
    • Encourage students to create a communication device that uses light and motion to transmit Morse code messages. Each position of the servo motor can represent a specific Morse code symbol, and the light sensor can interpret the incoming signals.
  1. Interactive Compass:
    • Challenge students to build a compass that responds to changes in light direction. The servo motor can act as a pointer, aligning itself with the direction of the light source. This project combines elements of science, technology, and navigation.
  1. Dance Party Light Show:
    • Have students create a dance party light show where the servo motor controls the movement of disco-like elements. The light sensor can be used to detect changes in ambient light, triggering different dance moves and light patterns.
  1. Sundial with a Modern Twist:
    • Task students with designing a modern sundial that not only tells time based on the position of the sun but also incorporates the servo motor to create dynamic shadow patterns as the day progresses.
  1. Interactive Science Fair Exhibit:
    • Allow students to choose a scientific concept they find interesting and build an interactive exhibit using the servo motor and light sensor to demonstrate the chosen concept. This could include models related to astronomy, physics, or environmental science.
  1. Creature Feature:
