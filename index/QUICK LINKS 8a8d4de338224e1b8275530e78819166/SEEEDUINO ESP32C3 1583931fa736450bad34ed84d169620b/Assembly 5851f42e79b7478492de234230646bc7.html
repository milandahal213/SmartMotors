Assembly

The Smart Motor box using ESP32C3 is the current prototype that checks most of our boxes. We build our PCB from Seeed Studio in China. This helps us keep the price lower and accelerate the assembly process.

Printed Circuit Board

Seeed Studio asks for gerber files, instructions and bill of materials which you can find below.

  1. Go https://www.seeedstudio.com/fusion_pcb.html . You might have to sign up and log in.
  1. Upload gerber files
  1. Upload instructions
  1. Upload bill of materials

  1. M2 screw, 10 mm
  1. M2 screw, 20 mm
  1. M2 threaded insert

  1. Grove light sensor

Preparing for Assembly

You will need to some preparation before assembling the parts.

  1. First of all, 3D print all the parts from above.
  1. Insert the threaded inserts (from the list of materials) into the pilot holes on the 3D printed parts for M2 bolts.
  1. Laser cut all the parts from the list above.
  1. Attach the servo motor (from the list of materials) to the side that is marked Back.
  1. You may nor may not need to solder the screen. In the current version, you will need to solder the screen (from the list of materials) to the board. Make sure you are soldering to the holes on the bottom row as shown in the image below.
  1. Connect the battery to the battery port.
  1. Sandwich the PCB between TOP and SUPPORT (from the 3D printing list) using 20mm M2 bolts and nuts.
  1. Connect the servo motor to the motor port as shown below. There are 4 pins but most of the servo come with 3 pins. It is important to connect the board as shown, ensuring the GND (usually the black wire) is in the inside.
  1. Use 4mm M2 bolts to connect the side panels together.
  1. Finally, connect the BOTTOM piece using 4mm M2 bolts.
